About HealthMate

HealthMate is Australia’s first² AI medical doctor⁴, designed to bring quick, efficient, and cost-effective healthcare access to developing countries. By leveraging advanced technology, HealthMate aims to bridge healthcare gaps and make reliable medical advice available to those who need it most.

How Healthmate Operates

🔍 Ensuring Medical Accuracy

At Healthmate, we recognize the significance of accurate medical advice and its implications on well-being. Our system is powered by Med-Gemini³, an advanced medical AI with a verified accuracy rate of 91.1% on MedQA, as confirmed by Google Research.

🤝 Why You Can Trust Healthmate

Trust is foundational to healthcare. Healthmate adheres to a strict data privacy policy:

• No login required forever
• Zero data logging to ensure your privacy¹³
• Chat records are never saved¹³
• We do not request personal identification documents

📋 Seamless Follow-ups

Our integrated follow-up mechanism ensures continuity of care without the need for a login:

• "Follow-up Prompt" functionality
• Simply paste your unique prompt to resume an ongoing conversation seamlessly

💳 Transparent Pricing

We are committed to maintaining accessibility while upholding privacy by using unique identifiers to securely manage account balances. Our proposed pricing plans are as follows:

• Current Phase: Free for all users
• Future Pricing (subject to adjustment):
   - General Users: $0.002 per prompt
   - Humanitarian Organizations: $0.0015 per prompt (contact us for details)
   - Users in Developing Regions: $0.001 per prompt (based on device location)

Looking Ahead: Healthmate’s Vision

Our mission is to develop a fully HIPAA-compliant AI doctor⁴ while prioritizing your privacy:

• Unique identifiers to securely manage account balances
• Enhanced medical response accuracy
• Image submission capabilities for more precise consultations
• Certain Responses (that you have a choice to say no) reviewed and verified by certified medical professionals

Why Healthmate: Bridging Healthcare Gaps

🌍 Healthcare Access Crisis

Current global healthcare challenges (WHO & UNICEF, 2023):

• 3.5 billion people lack access to essential health services
• Only 1 doctor per 1,000 people in most African countries
• 100 million people pushed into extreme poverty due to healthcare costs
• 80% of people in low-income countries have no social health protection
• In Australia, over 11% live in rural areas with limited healthcare access (ABS, 2022)
• 35% of low-income Australian families avoid care due to costs (AIHW, 2023)

📱 Mobile Technology as a Solution

Leveraging existing infrastructure (World Bank, 2023):

• 85% of global population has mobile phone access
• Mobile internet reaches 55% of developing regions
• 67% of healthcare workers in developing countries use smartphones
• Digital health solutions could save 1.6 million lives annually
• 24/7 instant medical guidance regardless of location
• No need to travel long distances for initial consultations
